Lectures 

Lecture 1: Introduction, overview and global properties of the Interstellar Medium

1. Ecology of the Interstellar Medium

Topics: Class organization and introduction. Course overview. Discovery of the ISM and history of ISM research. Nebulae: HII regions, reflection nebulae, SNRs, dark clouds. ISM phases and their properties. Cycle of material between phases. Energy sources and energy densities in the ISM.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 1

Weblecture: hour 1 & hour 2

Lecture 2: Interaction of radiation with interstellar matter

2. Radiative processes

Topics: Lack of (Local) Thermal Equilibrium. Statistical equilibrium. Excitation temperature. Description of the radiation field: specific intensity, flux density, specific energy density, photon occupation number. Definition of the Einstein coefficients for absorption, spontaneous emission and stimulated emission. Relation between the Einstein coefficients. Line profiles. Natural broadening and Doppler broadening. Equation of transfer for radiation. Relation of emissivity and absorption coefficient to Einstein coefficients. Optical depth and source function. Kirchhoff's law. Population inversion and masers.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 6 except Section 6.7; Draine, Ch. 7

Weblecture: hour 1 & hour 2 & hour 3 & hour 4

Lecture 3: The atomic interstellar medium

3. The Atomic Interstellar Medium

Topics: The HI 21cm line. Equation of transfer. Spin temperature. Optical depth of the HI 21cm line. Deriving HI column density and mass from optically thin HI emission. HI absorption. Emission-absorption observations and the evidence for the 2-phase ISM. Optical/UV absorption lines. The curve of growth. Lyman-alpha absorption. The Lyman-alpha forest and damped Lyman-alpha absorbers.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 8 and 9 except Sections 9.6 and 9.8

Weblecture: hour 1 & hour 2

Lecture 4: Photoionization, and recombination lines

4. Ionization and recombination

Topics: Ionization processes: photoelectric absorption. Recombination processes: radiative recombination. The hydrogen spectrum. Recombination lines. Case A and case B recombination spectra. Measuring star formation using recombination lines.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 13 except Sections 13.2 and further; Draine, Ch. 14 except Sections 14.2.4 and further

Weblecture: hour 1 & hour 2

Lecture 5: HII regions

5. HII Regions

Topics: HII regions. Strömgren spheres. Intensity of recombination lines and radio continuum. Emission measure. Deriving ionizing photon production rate and star formation rate. Ionization of hydrogen, helium, and heavier elements. Structure and evolution of HII regions. Dust in HII regions.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 15 except Sections 15.4, 15.7, and 15.8; also Draine, Ch. 10, only Section 10.5

Weblecture: hour 1 &  hour 2

Lecture 6: Collisional excitation

6. Collisional excitation and nebular diagnostics

Topics: Collisional excitation. Collision strength. Critical density for a 2-level system. Behaviour of the population ratio in limiting cases of very high and very low density. Implications for HI spin temperature. Generalization to multi-level systems. Line ratios as diagnostics for density, temperature and abundance.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 17 except Section 17.7; Draine, Ch. 18 except Sections 18.4, 18.6 and 18.7, qualitatively only; also Draine Section 2.3

Weblecture: hour 1 & hour 2

Lecture 7: Molecules and molecular spectra

7. Molecules and molecular spectra

Topics: Molecular energy levels: Born-Oppenheimer approximation, electronic, vibrational and rotational transitions; spectra of diatomic molecules.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 5 (only global properties of spectra of diatomic molecules) except Sections 5.1.6 and further.

Weblecture: hour 1 & hour 2

Lecture 8: Molecular clouds

8. Molecular clouds

Topics: Ortho- and para-H2. Molecules as probes of density and temperature. Molecular clouds. PDRs. H2 and CO as probes of molecular gas. Formation and destruction of H2. Self-shielding. 

Literature: Draine, Ch. 5 Sections 5.1.6 and 5.1.7; Draine, Ch. 31 except Sections 31.6-31.8.

Weblecture: hour 1 & hour 2

Lecture 9: Thermal balance

9. Thermal balance

Topics: Thermal balance of the ISM. Heating and cooling of HII regions. HII region temperatures. Heating and cooling of the neutral ISM and the 2-phase model. Heating and cooling of molecular clouds.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 27 except Section 27.2; Draine Ch. 30.

Weblecture: hour 1 & hour 2

Lecture 10: Interstellar dust

10. Interstellar dust

Topics: Interstellar dust. Extinction curves and reddening; features on the extinction curve. Definitions of absorption, scattering and extinction cross sections. Constraints on dust models. Constituents of interstellar dust. PAHs. The MRN dust model. Radiative heating and cooling of dust grains. Steady state temperatures. Temperature spikes in very small dust grains. Infrared emission.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 21, except Sections 21.3, 21.4, 21.6 and 21.8; Draine Ch. 22.1, only first 4 definitions and discussion around Eq. 22.6; Draine Ch. 23 except Sections 23.4, 23.6 - 23.9 and 23.10.1; Draine Ch. 24 except Sections 24.1.2 and 24.4

Weblecture: hour 1 & hour 2

Lecture 11: Molecular clouds and their properties

11. Molecular clouds and their properties

Topics: Radiative trapping. Solving excitation and radiative transfer for very optically thick lines: escape probabilities. Using CO as a tracer of the mass of molecular clouds. The X-factor. Global properties of molecular clouds.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 19; Draine Ch. 32 except Sections 32.5 - 32.8 and 32.10.

Weblecture: hour 1 & hour 2

Lecture 12: Shocks, and the 3-phase model of the ISM

12. Shocks and the 3-phase ISM

Topics: Shock waves. Shocks in molecular clouds. Supernova remnants. Hot gas and the 3-phase model of the ISM.

Literature: Draine, Ch. 36, except Sections 36.4 and 36.5 (only the global points, skip mathematical details); Draine, Ch. 39, qualitative only; skip Section 39.3 

Weblecture:  hour 1  &  hour 2
